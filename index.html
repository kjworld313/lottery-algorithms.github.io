Introduction

Lottery Algorithms in Digital Games

Lottery algorithms, also commonly referred to as “RNG” or Random Number Generators though typically more complex, are controversial systems that developers implement in order to create random probability games which reward a user based on fulfillment of certain conditions. Across the world, there are countless slot machines in airports, casinos, and resorts. With the advancement of technology, the gambling industry is accessible on digital platforms with apps like Jackpot Party Casino Slots and Fortune Tiger. Now, players can interact with lottery algorithms anytime and anywhere.

Loot boxes (League of Legends and Destiny 2) and gacha (Genshin Impact and Arknights) are two examples of lottery algorithms in games. Various games, such as Azur Lane and Honkai: Star Rail, use a combination of types. Companies market the algorithms by incentivizing users with advantageous rewards such as real currency, cool characters, and other desirable items to encourage user engagement. Most players are unaware of the algorithm’s design, and the winnings are usually not directly proportional to the money players invest in beating it. Developers design lottery algorithms with mechanisms or features meant to keep players engaged with the game and spending money to earn additional plays.
