Solution Options

Option 1

Do not allow lottery algorithms such as gacha, loot boxes, or virtual slot machines in digital games.

Impacts

  • Creators’ right to deploy digital games is maintained. Players’ and streamers’ rights to experience and promote safe gameplay is protected.
  • Developers can better align to codes of ethics or ethical standards.
  • Reduces psychological and financial risks for the player base and their acquaintances
  • Creators cannot incorporate randomness into their games as a result and must utilize other gameplay components.

Option 2

Allow lottery algorithms in digital games with regulations on player age, algorithm design, and in-game warnings or public service announcements.

Impacts

  • Creators’ rights to distribute lawful content are protected.
  • All players and streamers must meet requirements to play and are treated fairly regarding warnings and resource access.
  • Creators retain freedom to create digital games with random events and profit from in-app purchases, while players and streamers can still play or promote the types of games they enjoy with reduced risks.
  • Regulations cannot be fully proportionate to financial or mental status. Regulations could frustrate susceptible and possessive players, resulting in a reduced player base for creators.

Option 3

Allow lottery algorithms in digital games without in-app “pull” purchases.

Impacts

  • All players can still engage with games they enjoy and choose to interact with the algorithms. Streamers can create content with reduced legal risk. Creators can still incorporate lottery algorithms and randomness into digital games.
  • Players with varying financial means have equal ability to acquire "rolls" and win rewards.
  • While creators’ revenue is potentially restricted, they can still profit through other in-app purchases or advertisements.
  • Interest in digital casino slots may sink, as money from players finances the rewards. The digital gambling industry may suffer.

Option 4

Allow lottery algorithms without regulations.

Impacts

  • The rights of companies to create games and players to play them is protected. The right of players’ to experience transparent, fair, and safe gameplay is not respected.
  • Creators can design algorithms to be highly addictive, lucrative, and unfair. Some players may have more opportunities to acquire better items, which accelerates their gameplay compared to those without financial backing.
