Discussion Questions

  1. Do not allow lottery algorithms such as gacha, loot boxes, or virtual slot machines in digital games.
    • The Rights Lens: creators’ right to deploy digital games is maintained and players’ and streamers’ rights to experience and promote safe gameplay is protected.
    • The Virtue Lens: developers can better align to codes of ethics or ethical standards.
    • The Communitarian Lens: psychological and financial risks for the player base, their family members, and other acquaintances are reduced.
    • Potential pitfalls: Creators cannot incorporate randomness into their games as a result and must utilize other gameplay components.
  2. Allow lottery algorithms in digital games with regulations on design and age of players, probability and design transparency, in-app purchases, or and in-game warnings or PSAs on ethical issues and how to recognize problematic gambling behaviors and addiction.
    • The Right Lens: creators’ rights to distribute lawful content are protected and players’ right to play and have access to support resources is protected.
    • The Justice Lens: all players and streamers must meet equal requirements to play and are treated fairly regarding warnings and resource access.
    • The Care Ethics Lens: creators retain freedom to create digital games with random events and profit from in-app purchases, while players and streamers can still play or promote the types of games they enjoy with reduced risks.
    • Potential pitfalls: regulations are not proportionate to financial or mental status and players could ignore PSAs and warnings. Regulations could frustrate susceptible and possessive players, resulting in a reduced player base for creators.
  3. Allow lottery algorithms in digital games without in-app “pull” purchases.
    • The Communitarian Lens: all players can still engage with games they enjoy and choose to interact with the algorithms, streamers can create content with reduced legal risk, and creators can still incorporate lottery algorithms and randomness into digital games.
    • The Justice Lens: players with varying financial means have equal chances to acquire chances and win rewards with regards to financial status.
    • The Utilitarianism Lens: while creators’ revenue is potentially restricted, they can still profit through other in-app purchases or advertisements.
    • Potential pitfalls: interest in digital casino slots may sink, as money from players finances the rewards. The digital gambling industry may suffer.
  4. Allow lottery algorithms without regulations.
    • The Rights Lens: the rights of companies to create games and players to play them is protected. The right of players’ to experience transparent, fair, and safe gameplay is not respected.
    • The Justice Lens: creators can design algorithms to be highly addictive, lucrative, and unfair. Some players may have more opportunities to acquire better items, which accelerates their gameplay compared to those without financial backing.
